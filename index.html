EDEN: Electrified Distributed Energy Network Tree Campaign

The EDEN initiative—Electrified Distributed Energy Network—invites churches, neighborhood groups, and founder-donors to sponsor a “solar tree node” in our network. These solar trees, arranged like structural canopies, collect sunlight and store energy for flexible use in homes, businesses, or electric vehicle charging.

Why EDEN?

A faith-and-community aligned launch strategy to seed the DER network in places that say “yes” fastest.

What It Funds

Structural solar tree canopies, lithium-ion battery storage systems, and on-site EV charging service loads.

Where It Goes

Cities in Texas and DeKalb County (Decatur, Georgia, USA) are the first deployment zones — and the model is replicable nationwide.

How This Becomes a Virtual Power Plant

We aggregate many small, tree-like distributed energy resource (DER) nodes — each featuring photovoltaic panels, battery storage, and vehicle-to-grid capable EVs — and a market-based controller dispatches power dynamically every few minutes. This operational pattern is rooted in “Power Management in Advanced Life-Support Systems Using Market-Based Control,” originally developed at NASA Ames, and scaled here for civilian grid services.

As we expand, we match the business model found in the National Renewable Energy Laboratory (NREL) studies of DER aggregation to monetize flexibility and grid services — allowing us to operate without traditional utility-scale capital ownership.
